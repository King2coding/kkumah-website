About

I work at the University of Arizona on remote sensing, GIS, and AI for weather and climate applications—spanning precipitation, snow/ice, and geospatial data fusion. I collaborate with the NASA GPCP team on AVHRR‑based precipitation algorithms while contributing to broader UArizona research initiatives.

My background includes developing ML‑based rainfall retrievals that blend satellite (e.g., MSG SEVIRI) with commercial microwave link data, and building reproducible Python pipelines for near‑real‑time mapping.

About Me

I am a remote sensing and geoinformatics researcher with expertise in satellite precipitation retrieval, GIS, and AI-based Earth observation. My research spans rainfall and snow mapping, uncertainty evaluation, and the operationalization of algorithms into real-world applications. I collaborate closely with NASA’s GPCP team, TU Delft, Rainboo, and TAHMO while conducting independent research at the University of Arizona.

Current & Past Activities

Currently

  • • AVHRR-based global precipitation mapping at UArizona—algorithm development, evaluation and uncertainty diagnostics, in collaboration with the NASA GPCP team.
  • • “Buienradar for Africa” (Ghana first) with Rainboo, TU Delft and TAHMO—operationalising my PhD algorithm by blending satellite, CML and station data for near‑real‑time maps, nowcasts and alerts.
  • • Cryosphere data records—retrospective global snow & ice mapping and open data releases.

Previously

  • • ML precipitation retrievals integrating MSG SEVIRI with CMLs; automated Python workflows for near‑real‑time estimation.
  • • Visiting Scientist with EUMETSAT/H‑SAF, contributing to satellite precipitation retrieval R&D and validation.
  • • GIS & EO applications across agriculture and water (e.g., irrigation advisory pilots).
