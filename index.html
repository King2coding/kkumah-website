About

A scientist working where weather, water, and satellites meet.

Dr. Kwabena Kingsley Kumah is a remote sensing scientist and precipitation retrieval specialist focused on satellite-based precipitation estimation, high-latitude retrieval algorithms, snow and ice climate records, and multi-source evaluation of operational precipitation products.

His research bridges algorithm development, satellite data analysis, physical modeling, machine learning, and product validation across environments ranging from sub-Saharan Africa to global polar regions.

At the University of Arizona, he contributes to NASA-funded precipitation research, including AVHRR-based high-latitude precipitation algorithm development, diagnostic evaluation, bias correction, uncertainty analysis, and validation activities relevant to GPCP and future satellite precipitation records.

Science portfolio

Original contributions with practical climate-data impact.

AVHRR High-Latitude Precipitation

Lead development and diagnostic evaluation of an AVHRR-based precipitation retrieval product for global regions poleward of 55 degrees latitude.

Limb Darkening Correction

Developed scan-angle bias correction for AVHRR brightness temperatures using latitude, season, cloud type, and surface-type dependencies.

GMASI Snow & Ice Extension

Extended the Global Merged Atmospheric Surface Index snow and ice cover record back to 1980-1987 using machine learning and reanalysis-based retrospective mapping.

Ocean Precipitation Validation

Developing a multi-reference validation framework for GPCP, IMERG, ERA5, and MERRA-2 against complementary in-situ references.

Antarctic Snowfall Reassessment

Lead analyst for basin-scale Antarctic snowfall reassessment using GRACE mass-budget constraints across IMBIE drainage basins.

CML-Satellite Rainfall Retrieval

Developed rainfall estimation algorithms combining Commercial Microwave Links with MSG SEVIRI observations for rainfall monitoring in Africa.
