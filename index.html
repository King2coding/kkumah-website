About

Postdoctoral Research Scholar, Department of Hydrology & Atmospheric Sciences

Dr. Kwabena Kingsley Kumah is a remote sensing scientist and precipitation retrieval specialist whose work focuses on satellite-based precipitation estimation, high-latitude retrieval algorithms, snow and ice climate records, and multi-source evaluation of operational precipitation products.

His research bridges algorithm development, satellite data analysis, physical modeling, machine learning, and product validation. He works with infrared and microwave satellite observations, in-situ measurements, reanalysis datasets, and geospatial machine learning methods to improve precipitation estimates across challenging environments, from sub-Saharan Africa to the global polar regions.

At the University of Arizona, he contributes to NASA-funded precipitation research, including AVHRR-based high-latitude precipitation algorithm development, diagnostic evaluation, and validation activities relevant to the Global Precipitation Climatology Project (GPCP) and future satellite precipitation records.

His broader work also includes commercial microwave link rainfall retrieval, satellite–ground data fusion, ocean precipitation evaluation, and Antarctic precipitation benchmarking using mass-balance constraints.

Original Scientific Contributions

AVHRR-Based High-Latitude Precipitation

Lead development and diagnostic evaluation of an AVHRR-based precipitation retrieval product for global regions poleward of 55° latitude.

Limb Darkening Correction Algorithm

Developed a correction for scan-angle-dependent AVHRR brightness temperature bias using latitude, season, cloud type, and surface-type dependencies.

GMASI Snow & Ice Climate Data Record Extension

Extended the Global Merged Atmospheric Surface Index snow and ice cover record back to 1980–1987 using machine learning and reanalysis-based retrospective mapping.

IEPPO Ocean Precipitation Validation Framework

Developing a multi-reference validation framework for ocean precipitation products including GPCP, IMERG, ERA5, and MERRA-2 against complementary in-situ references.

Antarctic Snowfall Reassessment

Lead analyst for basin-scale Antarctic snowfall reassessment using GRACE mass-budget constraints across IMBIE drainage basins.

CML–Satellite Rainfall Retrieval

Developed rainfall estimation algorithms combining Commercial Microwave Links with MSG SEVIRI satellite observations for high-resolution rainfall monitoring in Africa.
