Hi, I'm Dr. Kingsley Kumah

Remote Sensing | GIS | AI Applications

Postdoctoral Research Scholar at the University of Arizona. I specialize in remote sensing-based retrievals, geospatial analytics, and AI-driven Earth observation solutions. My work integrates satellite, reanalysis, and ground-based data to enhance rainfall, snow, and climate monitoring.

Current & Past Activities

Current Projects

  • 🔹 Developing an AVHRR-based global precipitation mapping algorithm with the NASA GPCP team, focusing on algorithm development and uncertainty evaluation.
  • 🔹 Operationalizing PhD algorithm in Africa with Rainboo, TU Delft, and TAHMO to blend satellite and commercial microwave link (CML) data for real-time rainfall monitoring, starting in Ghana.
  • 🔹 Building geospatial AI pipelines for snow and ice mapping to extend satellite climate records beyond the observational era.

Past Work

  • 🔹 Developed a machine learning-based rainfall estimation algorithm combining satellite and telecom data, improving rainfall monitoring in ungauged regions.
  • 🔹 Visiting Scientist with EUMETSAT/H-SAF, contributing to operational satellite precipitation retrieval and validation.
  • 🔹 PhD research at University of Twente focused on integrating CML data and MSG SEVIRI satellite observations for rainfall retrieval in Africa.
